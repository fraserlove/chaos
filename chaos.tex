\begin{abstract}
    \noindent A topological dynamical system is comprised of a continuous mapping acting on a compact metric space. Topological dynamical systems often give rise rise to complex and chaotic behaviour. This project examines various definitions of chaos, specifically covering Devaney chaos, Li-Yorke chaos and topological chaos. We will explore how these definitions apply to topological dynamical systems on the interval, the unit circle, in sequence space and on compact countable sets. We shall cover important topological properties of chaos, such as topological transitivity, sensitive dependence, dense periodic points, scrambled sets, Li-Yorke pairs and topological entropy. Finally the text shall conclude by characterising various chaotic systems and compairing the various definitions of chaos.
\end{abstract}
